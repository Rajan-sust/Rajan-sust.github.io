Bangladesh Mathematical Olympiad
Solution (National Round)
Problem:
Find all possible non-negative integer solution \((x,y)\) of the following equation: $$ x! + 2^y = (x+1)! $$ (Category: Secondary; 2023)
Solution:
We know, \begin{align*} n! &= n \times (n-1) \times \dots \times 3 \times 2 \times 1\\ &= n \times (n-1)! \end{align*} Given that, \begin{align*} x! + 2^y &= (x+1)! \\ \implies 2^y &= (x+1)! - x! \\ \implies 2^y &= (x+1) \times x! - x! \\ \implies 2^y &= x! \times \{ (x+1) - 1 \} \\ \implies 2^y &= x! \times x \tag{1} \\ \end{align*}
From equation \(1\), it is clear that \(x \ngtr 2 \). When \(x\) is greater than \(2\), such as \(x = 5\), prime numbers \(3, 5\) emerge that cannot be represented as a power of \(2\).
Therefore, \((x, y) = \{(1, 0), (2, 2)\} \)
Problem:
For any positive integer \(n\), \(f(n)\) to be the smallest positive integer that does not divide \(n\). For example, \(f(1) = 2, f(6) = 4\). Prove that for any positive integer \(n\), either \(f(f(n))\) or \(f(f(f(n)))\) must be equal to \(2\).
(Category: Higher Secondary; 2023)
Solution:
$$ n = \begin{cases} 2k - 1 & \\ 2k & \end{cases} ; k \in \mathbb{N} $$ If \(n = 2k - 1\), \begin{align*} f(f(f(2k - 1))) &= f(f(2)) \\ &= f(3) \\ &= 2 \end{align*} If \(n = 2k \), then according to Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic [1]: $$ 2k = {P_1}^{n_1} \times {P_2}^{n_2} \times \dots \times {P_m}^{n_m} $$ where \(P_1, P_2, P_3, \dots \) denotes prime numbers \(2, 3, 5, \dots\) respectively and \(n_i\) denotes non-negative integer but \(n_m\) positive integer \begin{align*} f(f(2k)) &= f(f({P_1}^{n_1} \times {P_2}^{n_2} \times \dots \times {P_m}^{n_m})) \\ &= f(min({P_1}^{n_1 + 1}, {P_2}^{n_2 + 1}, \dots, {P_m}^{n_m + 1}, {P_{m+1}}^{1})) \\ &= f(P^x)\\ &= 2 \end{align*} Reference:
