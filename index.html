Rajan Saha Raju
Researcher | CS Graduate
rajansaha AT sust DOT edu

My research interests span Computational Linguistics and Bioinformatics. In Computational Linguistics, I focus on areas such as Text-to-Speech synthesis, Text Normalization, Homograph and Homophone Disambiguation, and Inverse Text Normalization.

I completed my B.Sc. in Computer Science and Engineering with distinction from Shahjalal University of Science and Technology (SUST), Bangladesh. During my time at SUST, I was a core member of the SUST Bangla Text-to-Speech (TTS) research group. Our team achieved national-level recognition by becoming the champions of the AI for Bangla contest for our Bangla TTS. Additionally, we have also published several research articles on Bangla TTS and Bangla text normalization.

After graduation, I joined Omics Lab, a research group comprising individuals from various disciplines, including molecular biology, genetic engineering, and computer science. During my time there, I was the primary author of a journal paper on virus taxonomy detection and co-authored a paper on the de novo assembly of the SARS-CoV-2 genome.

I have a deep passion for mathematics and am a former math olympiad contestant. I write solutions for problems from the national round of the Bangladesh Mathematical Olympiad (BdMO).
